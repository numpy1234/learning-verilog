Data flow uses continous assignments and the keyword assign

syntax:

assign <drive_strength> # <delay> <list of assignments>;

Example :
wire out;
assign out = var1 & var2;

LHS must be net. RHS can be net/reg


Example : 4 to 1 line multiplexer

module mux4x1(y, s0, s1, i0, i1, i2, i3);
  input i0, i1, i2, i3, s1, s0;
  output y;
  assign y= (~s1 & ~s0 & i0) | (~s1 & s0 & i1) | (s1 & ~s0 & i2) | (s1 & s0 & i3);
endmodule

Example: mux2x1
module mux2x1(y, sel, i0, i1);
  input i0, i1, sel;
  output y;
  assign y= sel ? i1 : i0;
endmodule



Example:
assign #10 out =in1 & in2;
delay in continous assign (10 units of time)
used in testbenches




Operators:

bitwise and - & (operates on each bit)
logical and - && (result is either 1 or 0, anything other than 0 is 1)
bitwise or - | (operates on each bit)
logical or - || (result is a single bit 0 or 1 , used in if and while condition)

^ = xor
~^ = xnor


assign y =^a  (this means it would do xor of all the bits of a and store the result in y )


>> right shift (right shift by 1 is divided by 2 mathematically but in hardware it may truncate the decimal)
<< left shift (left shift by 1 is multiplied by 2 but in hardware it depends on bit width as oveflow can happen)


{} concatenation
{{}} replication  {4{a}}

? : conditional
